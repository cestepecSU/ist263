About APA

Growing up my grandpa made the most amazing dishes.
Apa is from Negros in the Philippines. He grew up with 4 sisters and one brother, making him one of six.
He always loved to cook and found a passion for it when joining the Coast Guard where he cooked for all his crewmates.
Now he cooks for my family, making amazing dishes that I’ve always wanted to recreate.
Since living on my own, I have been recreating his dishes with his help.
The two recipes that I’ve included I was able to make from my grandfather’s instructions.
I hope you enjoy making this food as much as I have.

Apa’s Spaghetti

Sunday Night Spaghetti

Ingredients

  • Oil -enough to fill the bottom of the pan
  • Butter -1 stick
  • Garlic- 4 cloves
  • Onion- ½ large onion
  • Spaghetti
  • Salt- to taste
  • Pepper- to taste
  • Water
  • Ground Beef- 1 pound
  • Beef broth- 1 cup (add more to taste)
  • Tomato sauce- 1 can
  • Parmesan cheese- to top

Instructions
First make your spaghetti do this with salt in the water and olive oil.
Take your oil and butter, melt this in a large pan. Add your chopped garlic.
Fry your garlic then put the oil, butter garlic mixture aside.
Fry your onion in a separate pan and place this aside.
Place your beef in a pan with more oil and break it up.
Once it is separated then add all three together in one large pan.
Add a little water and the tomato sauce, stir to combine. Add beef bullion. Boil on low heat for at least one hour.
Taste your meat sauce when cooked.
When its finished cooking serve immediately atop your pasta, adding grated parmasean cheese.
Add garlic bread to your dish for your sauce.
Enjoy!

Appa's Eggplant Omelets

Tortang Talong (Filipino Eggplant Omelet)


Ingredients


  • Egglant
  • Ground Beef
  • Onion- 1 large
  • Butter- 3 tablespoons
  • Water
  • Salt- to taste
  • Pepper- to taste
  • Egg- 1
  • Beef- 1 pound

Instructions

1. Prepare the oven: Arrange a rack in the middle of the oven, then line it with foil. (Alternatively, place a baking sheet on the rack to catch any drips.) Heat the oven to 350°F.
2. Rinse and dry the eggplant: Rinse the eggplant under cool running water, then pat dry with a clean kitchen towel.
3. Bake the eggplant: Place the eggplant directly on the middle rack and bake until incredibly tender and the skin is wrinkly, 45 minutes to 1 hour.
4. Cool and peel: Use tongs to remove the eggplant to a cutting board or wire rack and let cool. Once cool enough to handle, cut the eggplant in half and use a spoon to scoop out the flesh.
Recipe
Heat a pan on low, add your butter and onion. Add ground beef and break it up.
Chop your onion and add it to your ground beef. Once cooked put this on the side.
Wisk your egg and set it to the side.
Add butter to a pan, once melted add your pealed eggplant and flatten it with the spatula
flatten it in a pan. and put the mixture of eggs and beef on top.
Flatten this out in a pancake shape. Cook on each side then remove from the pan and Enjoy!
